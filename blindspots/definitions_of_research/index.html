LASER PULSE
Research & Practicioner Partnership Toolkit

Blindspots in collaborations, discussion guides, & resouce materials to improve research partnerships

This toolkit is organized by blindspot, which is a potential area of conflict due to different perspectives of each person involved in a research partnership. Since researchers and practitioners have different world views, experiences, and needs these blindspots can occur. Each blindspot presents a caricature of what an academic researcher and a practitioner might say and a suggested convergence point, or perspective that can ease conflict and improve the partnership.

Convergence Point
Choose more applied researchers when deciding on research partners. Have clear research questions before the research project begins and before choosing research partners. Discuss ahead of time how potential negative findings will be handled as part of the partnering process. we both see donors as demanding.

Questions Researchers Can Ask
How do you plan to use this research? How does it complement or supplement programmatic (M&E) data collection?
How can we learn from potential negative findings from the research?
What is your experience with academic researchers?

Questions Practitioners Can Ask
What is the challenge or gap the research will address?
What are the research questions?
What is your experience with applied research?

Questions Both Can Ask
How can the expectations between us regarding research and its outcomes be aligned?
